Biography

Amiri Baraka was born in Newark, NJ on October 7, 1934. African-American poet, critic, and dramatist known for works such as Blues People: Negro Music in White America (nonfiction), The System of Dante’s Hell (fiction), A Black Mass (drama), and Slave Ship (poems). He served as Poet Laureate of New Jersey from 2002-2003. He appeared in Bulworth, a 1998 film that starred Warren Beatty as Senator Jay Billington Bulworth. He attended Rutgers University and Columbia University. He published his first poetic work, Preface to a Twenty Volume Suicide Note, in 1961.
