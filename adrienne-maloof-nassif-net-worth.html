Adrienne Maloof-Nassif, an American businesswoman, philanthropist, and television personality, estimated to have a net worth of $75 million, although her prenup back in 2013 suggested it protected her $300 million net worth. Maloof was also a cast member of Bravo's The Real Housewives of Beverly Hills, which is a part of the network's Real Housewives franchise.

She was born in Albuquerque, New Mexico, on September 4, 1961. She is the youngest of George and Coleen Maloof's three children. Adrienne traces her ancestry to Ireland and Lebanon. Her father is a multi-billionaire entrepreneur who has successfully extended the family business into lucrative banking, hotels, and trucking.

She developed an interest in the family business during her adolescence when her father was still the distributor of Coors beer. Adrienne was primarily responsible for the Maloof Companies' customer service standards in the 1990s. By the early 2000s, the Maloof family's net worth had surpassed $1 billion. After graduating from high school in the 1980s, she enrolled at the University of New Mexico on a tennis scholarship. She was a member of the Phi Beta Phi sorority at her university. In 1986, she earned a bachelor's degree in political science and began working as a marketing specialist for various local businesses.

She owns several fashion brands, the most famous example of these is "Adrienne Maloof by Charles Jordan," an exclusively Jordan-designed shoe collection. She is a co-owner of Maloof Companies' various business holdings. The Palms Casino Resort in Las Vegas, Nevada; the Sacramento Kings and Sacramento Monarchs; Maloof Productions and Maloof Music; and the annual Maloof Money Cup skateboarding event are among the business interests. Additionally, she sells a line of hangover supplements named Never Too Hungover. In 2010, she was confirmed as a series regular on Bravo's The Real Housewives of Beverly Hills. She appeared on the show for the first three seasons but did not return for the fourth and seventh. Since season five, she has gradually evolved into a supporting cast member. According to reports, she earned $200,000 per season of Real Housewives. She served as a judge on Miss Universe 2011 and has starred in the reality TV shows like Chocolate City and Hell's Kitchen.

She married Paul Nassif, a facial plastic surgeon who specializes in rhinoplasty, on May 2, 2002. Gavin (born 2003) and Collin and Christian (twins) are the couple's three sons (b. 2006). They lived in Beverly Park, a Los Angeles gated community. On July 30, 2012, Nassif filed for divorce from her. The papers, filed in Los Angeles Superior Court, stated that the separation was due to irreconcilable differences.

According to E! News, Maloof's divorce from her ex-husband and celebrity plastic surgeon Paul Nassif was civil, and an impenetrable prenup safeguarded both parties' substantial assets. Adrienne accused Dr. Paul of abuse and was awarded sole custody of the couple's three children. Additionally, the couple is attempting to sell their $12 million Los Angeles mansion.

Other than work, Maloof devotes a significant amount of time to philanthropy. She has volunteered for numerous charities, including School On Wheels, the Good News Foundation, and the Sacramento SPCA's Camp Kindness program. Maloof was working closely with Congress in 2019 to prevent school shootings. Maloof revealed in January 2013 that she was dating Sean Stewart, the son of British rocker Rod Stewart.
