American comedian, novelist, and actor Donald “Don” Jay Rickles was born in New York City. For his parts in “Run Silent, Run Deep” (1958), “Kelly’s Heroes” (1970), and “C.P.O. Sharkey,” Rickles rose to fame.

He has also received a Primetime Emmy Award throughout his career for the 2007 documentary “The Don Rickles Project.”

On April 6, 2017, Don Rickles passed away from kidney failure.

Early Life, Birthdate, Parents and Early Career of Don Rickles

On May 6, 1926, Don Rickles was born in New York City.

His parents, Max and Etta, were Jews. Rickles joined the American Navy after finishing high school and served there throughout World War 2. Rickles made the decision to concentrate on his acting career instead two years later.

In the 1950s, Rickles began his early career by performing stand-up comedy in various New York venues. The viewers adored what they saw, and Rickles went on to become well-known as a result.

Professional Career of Don Rickles

After graduating, Don Rickles began his career by landing tiny television jobs.

In 1958, Rickles made his film debut in the drama “Run Silent, Run Deep” after beginning stand-up comedy. Over the following few years, he started to make numerous appearances in drama series and sitcoms on television.

After his debut in 1965, Rickles made more than 100 appearances on “The Tonight Show,” “The Rick Van Dyke Show,” “The Adam’s Family,” and “The Mothers-in-law,” both as a host and a guest.

Also he received a significant role in the Clint Eastwood-starring film Kelly’s Heroes in the 1970s. After that, Rickles made even more appearances in comedy like “The Dean Martin Roasts” and “The Don Rickles Show” (1973).

In the 1980s, Rickles started sharing the stage with Steve Lawrence during shows in Las Vegas. In 1983, they both co-hosted shows including “Bleeps & Blunders” and “Foul-ups.” Don also contributed to the 1990 films Tales from the Crypt, Innocent Blood, Casino, Toy Story, and Dirty Work (1998).

Don enjoyed fresh possibilities in the twenty-first century, was honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame, and saw a surge in public interest in his film project. In 2007, Rickles published his own memoir, titled “Rickles Book.” The Don Rickles Project, a documentary based on his life, premiered at the 2007 New York Film Festival before airing on HBO.

Unfortunately, Rickles passed away in April 2017. Kidney failure was the cause of death.
