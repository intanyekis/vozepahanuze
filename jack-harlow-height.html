Jack Harlow Wikipedia

Jack Harlow is a Louisville, Kentucky-born American rapper. His career began in 2015 with the release of many EPs and mixtapes before being signed to Don Cannon and DJ Drama's record label Generation Now, an Atlantic Records offshoot, in 2018. Harlow's first huge success occurred in 2020 with the release of his track "What's Poppin." Harlow was named to XXL magazine's "2020 Freshman Class" before releasing his debut studio album, That's What They All Say (2020), which went platinum in the United States. In 2022, Harlow released his second album, Come Home the Kids Miss You, and its lead track, "First Class," earned his second Hot 100 number one and first to debut at the top. Harlow has been nominated for multiple awards from various organizations, including Top New Artist at the 2021 Billboard Music Awards. He was named Variety's "Hitmaker of the Year" in the same year, and he was named one of Forbes' 30 Under 30. Harlow is going to make his acting debut in Calmatic's 2023 recreation of the 1992 film White Men Can't Jump.   

Jack Harlow Biography

Jack Harlow biography: - Jackman Thomas Harlow was born in Louisville, Kentucky on March 13, 1998. He went to Atherton High School and Highland Middle School.
