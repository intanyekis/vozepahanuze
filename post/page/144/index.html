What is the significance of the two membranes which surround the lungs

published on July 09, 2024
What are the two membranes surrounding the lungs? The pleura includes two thin layers of tissue that protect and cushion the lungs. The inner layer (visceral pleura) wraps around the lungs and is stuck so tightly to the lungs that it cannot be peeled off. The outer layer (parietal pleura) lines the inside of the chest wall.
